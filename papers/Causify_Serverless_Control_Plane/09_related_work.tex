\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-work}

\paragraph{Authorization models.}
NIST SP 800-162 formalizes attribute-based access control \cite{nist_sp800162}. OPA and Cedar provide richer policy languages for complex authorization logic \cite{opa_docs,cedar_docs}. Zanzibar models relationship-based authorization at global scale \cite{zanzibar}. The pattern in this paper does not replace these systems. It focuses on a narrower integration problem: deriving tenant context once, materializing it as a contract, and injecting scope into services that should not be interpreting identity for themselves.

\paragraph{AWS SaaS architecture.}
The AWS SaaS Lens recommends control-plane/data-plane separation for multi-tenant SaaS systems \cite{aws_saas_lens}. Our contribution is a concrete instantiation of that advice for a production AI platform: a versioned identity contract, explicit actor/effective context, OpenAPI filtering, and schema-prefix injection into module and job execution paths.

\paragraph{OIDC and OAuth.}
The \code{/v2/me} endpoint resembles an OpenID Connect UserInfo endpoint \cite{oidc_core}. The \viewas{} mechanism resembles delegated context, but it is intentionally narrower than general OAuth delegation \cite{oauth2}. We did not need a universal identity framework. We needed a precise platform contract that support workflows and service calls could consume consistently.

\paragraph{Service mesh enforcement.}
Service meshes such as Istio and Linkerd can enforce network-level authorization \cite{istio_authz,linkerd_policy}. They are useful, but network authorization is not enough for module-level tenant policy, OpenAPI surface filtering, or PostgreSQL schema-prefix injection. We keep those decisions at the application boundary where tenant semantics are visible.

\paragraph{Serverless and container execution.}
Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB, EKS, and Fargate are standard cloud primitives \cite{aws_lambda_docs,aws_api_gateway,dynamodb_docs,eks_docs,fargate_docs}. The paper is not claiming novelty in the primitives. The useful part is the boundary between them: serverless functions for short governance decisions and containers for long-running execution.
