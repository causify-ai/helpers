\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The hardest bugs in multi-tenant platforms are often small enough to look harmless. A scope field comes from the wrong place. A generated spec shows too much. A worker accepts a tenant hint it should never have trusted. A support session loses track of the real actor. The platform still runs, but trust leaks out of it.

The control-plane/data-plane split described here is a way to stop that leak. The control plane materializes identity, effective context, module surface, impersonation state, and tenant scope. The data plane runs the work. That separation kept governance reviewable across 12 services, reduced endpoint exposure for limited tenants, moved routine tenant changes out of deployments, and made incident analysis less dependent on memory.

The thesis is intentionally plain: governance is part of the product. Put it behind a boundary, make it explicit, and keep it boring enough to operate. The data plane can be ambitious. The control plane should be calm.
