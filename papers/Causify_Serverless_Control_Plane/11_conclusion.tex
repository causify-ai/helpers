\section{Conclusion}
  \label{sec:conclusion} In multi-tenant platforms, the expensive bugs
  are rarely clever. They are mundane: a missing scope field, a stale spec,
  an endpoint that exists when it should not, a job that runs under the wrong
  context. The platform fails not because compute is hard, but because governance
  becomes ambiguous.

  This paper described a production pattern that makes ambiguity unwelcome:
  a \emph{serverless control plane} that materializes identity,
  effective context, capability surface, and tenant scoping into
  explicit contracts, paired with a \emph{container data plane} that
  executes workloads without owning policy.

  The results are practical:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item tenancy becomes explicit (actor vs.\ effective context),

    \item scope is injected rather than guessed (\texttt{schema\_prefix}
      and friends),

    \item surface drift is reduced (filtered OpenAPI + module gating),

    \item and "who am I?" becomes cheap enough to ask often (ETag + conditional
      requests).
  \end{itemize}

  The thesis is simple:
  \begin{quote}
    \textbf{Governance is a product feature. Put it in one place, make
    it explicit, and keep it boring.}
  \end{quote}

  The data plane can remain ambitious. The control plane should remain calm.
  In a world where platforms grow faster than documentation, calm is a competitive
  advantage.
